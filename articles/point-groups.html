Point Groups

Molecules can be classified by their collection of symmetry elements called a point group. Here are all of the important point groups:



Point Group Symmetry Operations Examples Notes

`C_1`

E

Lowest form of symmetry that exists

`C_s`

σ

`C_i`

I

`C_(∞v)`

Linear molecule with σ alongside rotation axis

For linear molecules with no inversion center

`D_(∞h)`

Linear molecule with I, `C_2` & `σ` perpendicular to rotation axis.

Generally, linear molecules with an inversion center

`T_d`

`4 C_3, 3 C_2, 3 S_4, 6 σ_d`

Generally tetrahedrals

`O_h`

`4 C_3, 3 C_4, I`

Generally octahedral molecules

`I_h`

`6 C_5`

Highest form of symmetry possible
