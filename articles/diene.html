Explanation of Diene Joke

In organic chemistry, organic molecules containing a double bond are named with a suffix -ene. Molecules containing two double bonds are -dienes, di- meaning two. All of the molecules below are examples of dienes.

The joke in the scenario is that organic chemistry is usually considered a difficult class. Thus, after the organic chemistry exam, the student proclaims that he/she is dying. Instead of dying, however, the student is "diene," which is a homophone for dying. This is made more humorous by the fact that the diene molecule presented is akin to a student laying on his/her back.
